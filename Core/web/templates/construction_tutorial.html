Tube Tail Construction Tutorial

Step 1: Materials Preparation

Gather all your materials and tools before starting:

  • Ripstop nylon fabric
  • Sewing machine or needle and thread
  • Sharp scissors
  • Measuring tape
  • 2 carabiners
  • Iron and pressing surface
Pro Tip: Press your fabric before cutting to ensure it's flat and wrinkle-free.

Step 2: Cutting the Fabric

Cut your fabric according to the pattern dimensions:

Dimensions:

Warning: Don't forget to add seam allowance to all edges!

Step 3: Sewing the Tube

Sew the long edges together with right sides facing:

  1. Place fabric with right sides together
  2. Sew along the long edge using a ¼ inch seam
  3. Leave a 4-inch opening for turning
  4. Trim seams to ⅛ inch and clip curves
Pro Tip: Use a zigzag stitch or overlock stitch for better durability.

Step 4: Turning and Pressing

Turn the tube right side out and press it flat:

  1. Turn the tube through the opening
  2. Use a dowel or chopstick to push out corners
  3. Press the entire tube with a hot iron
  4. Topstitch close to the edge to reinforce

Step 5: Adding Reinforcements

Add reinforcement patches at the attachment points:

  1. Mark the positions at 25% and 75% of the length
  2. Fold fabric to create double layers at these points
  3. Sew through all layers with a reinforced stitch
  4. Attach carabiners to the reinforced sections
Pro Tip: Use a triple stitch or backstitch for extra strength at reinforcement points.

Step 6: Final Assembly

Complete the final touches:

  1. Close the opening with a hidden stitch
  2. Press the entire tube one final time
  3. Test that carabiners move freely
  4. Attach to your kite line and test fly!
Pro Tip: Test fly in light winds first to ensure everything works properly.
